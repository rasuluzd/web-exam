Technology and Society for Programmers

Image by Bernard Marr

Written by Michael Jordan

A law banning the use of AI in political campaigns may drive the development of more sophisticated AI to circumvent detection, making it harder to differentiate between genuine and AI-generated content. One could also say that banning AI campaigning is infringing on your freedom of speech by limiting your freedom of expression. Politicians who use AI campaigning as a tool for policies will now be compromised as shown by the source below. A blank ban could hinder technological progress and innovation. It will be the voters who will benefit from this law as they lessen the risk of misinformation and deepfakes. This law can also lead to a general increase in trust throughs the politicians and the government due to the lessened risk of false information. A ban on a type of technology may lead to loss of potential jobs, which could both be making AI campaigning or

Cybersecurity against said technology. Implementing this law would require significant resources for monitoring and enforcing the law. Examples may be developing robust AI detection mechanisms. The law should be accompanied by public education such as school education or public education campaigns to inform voters.
My first impression is that the Norwegian government is right in their concern with their proposal for regulation of AI , and the potential influence it can have the parliamentary election that is being held in 2025. The build up towards 2024 election in the US confirms the suspicions towards unregulated AI-usage and I think something must be done in that regard. At the very least, should we should have some form of regulation so that we can, at the very least, know what is AI-generated and what is not.
